    Three Gorges Dam

    The Three Gorges Dam in (Hubei province, China) is the largest power generating in the world by far. Spanning more than 2.3 kilometers across the Yangtze River, the third longest river in the world, the dam is 181 meters in height and can hold more than 39 billion cubic-meters (roughly 10 trillion gallons) of water. [1] Construction of the dam began in late 1994, and the dam body was completed in 2006 (shown on the right), though the last generator was not installed until 2012.
